Building My Website

Welcome to the development of my website! In this post, I'll be sharing my experience building my website from scratch using HTML and CSS.

The Beginning

Very early on in my cybersecurity journey after consulting with multiple people in the industry, there was one piece of advice that I was given over and over again. The advice was to create an online presence that showcases your interests and skills related to cybersecurity.

I took on this advice and decided I would create a blog where I can share my projects and thoughts, and also use it as an opportunity to learn more about cybersecurity in a more practical way.

So without any experiance whatsoever with building websites, I decided that my first blog entry should be about the process of creating my blog site from scratch.

The Process

I started off by watching a ground up web development YouTube series by LearnCode.Academy: Building a Website from scratch and once I got my first taste of editing a live server with my own code, I was hooked.

I quickly picked up the basics of html and css, and was able to start piecing together my own website.

I created a style plan for my blog, by visiting various blog sites and taking inspiration from the ones I liked best. I cut and pasted different styles onto a word document which formed the base template for my site.

After a few days of coding with plenty of formatting issues and hours of trial and error, I eventually had a basic structure ready to be used as V1 of my blog. The next challenge was going to be uploading my site to the web, which I decided I would do through GitHub Pages.
